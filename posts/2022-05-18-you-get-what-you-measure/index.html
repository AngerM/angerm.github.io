You get what you measure

18 May 2022

One of the biggest things a lot of teams and companies make the mistake with are their OKRs and metrics they use to measure their employees. This manifests itself in quite a few ways. Whatever metrics you choose to measure (a team or person), are going to be gamed and focued to the expense of everything else. When you are small, it's easy to correct and get your team focused instead on doing what is "best" for your company and its customers, but at scale your OKRs + perf metrics become the focus of people trying to get ahead. They are going to target ever thing they work on and assosciate with to things that help them sell the story that they deserve that promotion + greatly exceeds rating. because that is what you have told them is the most important to the company.

If you say that these metrics aren't whats most important, than whats the point of having them?

This is the catch-22 when it comes to picking what metrics you are going to choose to focus on.
